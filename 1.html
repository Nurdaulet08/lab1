Travel to Pavlodar

Pavlodar (Kazakh and Russian: Павлодар) is a city in northeastern Kazakhstan and the capital of Pavlodar Region. It is located 450 km northeast of the national capital Nur-Sultan, and 405 km southeast of the Russian city of Omsk along the Irtysh River. As of 2010, the city has a population of 331,710. The population of Pavlodar is composed predominantly of ethnic Kazakhs and Russians with significant Ukrainian, German and Tatar minorities. The city is served by Pavlodar Airport.

Interesting places

Mosque named after Mashkhur Zhusup

The Mashkhur Jusup Mosque (Kazakh: Máshhúr Júsip meshiti) is located in the center of Pavlodar within an area of 6 ha and is one of the largest mosques in Kazakhstan. It was opened after a year of construction in 2001 and can accommodate 1,500 worshipers. The mosque was named after the Kazakh poet and historian Mashkhur Jusup (1858–1931).

Annunciation Cathedral

The arches and vaults of the temple are built of red bricks. The temple has seven domes, symbolizing seven sacraments and seven ecumenical councils. Three more domes - the symbol of the Holy Trinity - are located on the belfry. The domes of the cathedral are sheathed with gold-colored metal coated with titanium nitride. The roof of the church was made in the city of Novosibirsk using a special technology from galvanized iron, covered with a polymer film. The main cross over the cathedral rises 51 meters from the ground. There are 9 bells in the temple (according to the number of angelic ranks). The largest bell weighs 1,024 kg, the smallest 4 kg.
